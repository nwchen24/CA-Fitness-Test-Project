About FITKIDS | SMARTKIDS

It is no secret that students need daily physical activity to support healthy growth and cognitive development, so an active school environment supporting daily physical activity, quality physical education, recess and sports participation is exactly what kids need to produce their academic best.

However, some schools may focus more on this aspect of schooling than others, so to help parents in assessing the relative fitness merits of schools in their country and local district this tool enables parents to identify schools that perform well both from a fitness perspective, as well as from an academic perspective (our tool reinforces the positive association between the two).

To do so FITKIDS |SMARTKIDS has painstaking compiled public school level results from two annual California-wide student testing programs to enable geographic cross discipline comparisons to be made.

In particular, the two testing programs are:

FitnessGram® (coordinated by the Cooper Institute), which provides a test battery assessing various health-related fitness components: aerobic capacity, body composition, and muscular strength, endurance, and flexibility).

California Assessment of Student Performance and Progress (CAASPP) System (coordinated by the California Department of Education), are tests performed from grade three to eleven across English language arts (ELA) and mathematics. The assessments which are delivered by computer consist of two sections: a computer-adaptive test and a Performance Task (PT) based on the Common Core State Standards for ELA and mathematics. The computer-adaptive section includes a range of items types such as selected response, constructed response, table, fill-in, graphing, etc. The PT are extended activities that measure a student’s ability to integrate knowledge and skills across multiple standards—a key component of college and career readiness.
